Games I've Helped Make

Throughout my time at RIT I have worked on multiple game projects utilizing different game engines and other programs.

The first game I worked on was for a project in my Game Development & Algorithmic Problem Solving class during the spring semester of 2025. The game is called "Make Every Day the Last". It's a 2D shooter with a top-down perspective in which you progress through three levels of enemies until you reach a final boss. The game featured saving progress, allowing players to keep the guns and upgrades they earned through gameplay. The game was a collaborative project between myself and three other people, working in the MonoGame engine. My work on the project includes all the player/weapon controls (movement, weapon orbit, shooting, parrying), shop functionality, the save system, and the creation of several assets. Throughout this experience, we ran playtests and incorporated feedback from testers in order to strengthen the game's functionality.

A game I just recently worked on is a 2D top-down perspective survival horror game called "". The game has the player trapped in a dark minseshaft with an angry monster hunting them down. In order to survive this monster, the player can hide in hinding spots scattered across the map. They can also find salt and place it at spots around the mineshaft to block the monster's path. They can also light lanterns along the way to make vision easier. For this project, my group worked with the Unity engine and utilized GitHub for version control. My work on the project includes the implementation and functionality of the lanterns and the salt placeables and collectibles and how the player interacts with all of these.
